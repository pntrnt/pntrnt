Hi all!,

For those who don’t know, I posted an article a while back about my life, and I thought I would,

1. Write it better then I did years ago.

& 2. Add to it with new things that have happened!

When I was sixteen years old, I had to have heart surgery. For about 4 months I had been having very odd cold/flu symptoms such as high temperatures, fatigue, night sweats, and joint/muscle aches. It was so weird, and I was diagnosed with many different things throughout the 4 months I was sick, as the doctors tired to figure out why I was never getting better.

I was diagnosed with the flu, with bronchitis, and pneumonia.

After heart surgery went successful, I was diagnosed with PTSD, generalized anxiety disorder, and major depression. I didn’t want to talk to anyone about it, but my mom still made me see a therapist, since I never wanted to stop sleeping on her floor.

Eventually, I got a new bed, and ended up sleeping in my own bed again with my puppy!

Through the years, my PTSD, anxiety and depression have gotten better, some days are better then others but thats just how life works. I still suck at social interaction and going out in public, but I think since I’ve met my boyfriend I’ve gotten a lot better with it.

After I graduated high school, I started working at a popular daycare, and at the start it was okay, but the bosses were very disrespectful of employees needs, and the children’s needs.

I wasn’t comfortable going in rooms with kids older then 3 years old, and my preferred age group is 4month-1year olds. But they still made me go in older aged levels, even though I wasn’t okay with it, and that wasn’t professional in anyway,

I ended up leaving that daycare just before my two years of being employed. For 2ish months after I quit that job, I was unemployed.

I thought about going back to school, but ended up going to work at a much smaller, family owned daycare, where I still am now. Of course, every place has their faults, but this place is very much better then my old place. They care about my mental health issues, and let me have off when I need it and don’t usually complain too much about me needing my time at points.

November 25th 2019, was probably the worst and best day of my life. I went on my first ever bumble date with a guy, and ended up hooking up. I thought he was playing me, and he wasn’t ever going to talk to me again. I felt so stupid. Little did I know, he’d be my boyfriend for the next 7 months and counting. He is the best thing to ever happen to me, and I’m pretty sure he’s the one I’m meant to be with for the rest of my life. He brings out the best in me, knows all of my faults and accepts them. I couldn’t ask for a better boyfriend. In March, my depression really came back as he had to move across the state because of the corona virus. I had to take 3 months off of work, and actually just went back on June first. I wasn’t sure if we’d be able to make it work long distance, but we have and I love him now more then ever.

The future

Obviously we can plan for the future, but never know if it’ll actually pan out the way you want them to.

My plans are, Fall of 2020 for my boyfriend to move in with my parents and I for his last semester of college. I feel that’ll be a great way to figure out if we can truly live together everyday.

When he graduates in December, I’ll move across the state away from my parents to live with him, and find a job OR go back to school to be an ultrasound technician. And thats as far as I have planned so far!

I hope you all enjoyed!
