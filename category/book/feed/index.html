Hi Love!!! How are you!!!

I hope that you enjoy this reflection that seems very accurate in the reality we currently live, I hope you like it:

‘The world is made of valuable details that deserve to be appreciated‘

In a world full of constant changes, everything seems to be going at a fast speed, without stopping, a modern progress interface, beneficial because each time we save more time or are more “connected” but ‘at what cost?‘

It is worrisome in some aspects of our life because I think it is something that makes us lose a bit of humanity every time, we lose the sense of appreciating, valuing, being curious, empathizing with our environment, with the people around us.

‘We must reconnect with ourselves, how?‘

It seems to me that even at times, we have to feel in the present moment, without thinking about what is to come, just, for a moment, to say: I am alive, now, I have to enjoy it, I am passing through.

‘How can we stop?‘

The key is to learn that Seeing, is not the same as Looking, it sounds ridiculous but it is real, think how many times you walk the same place, you know the way, you walk inertia, but, now think:

‘How many times you stopped to look beyond the usual?Few or nothing, right?‘

To look is to see beyond, it is to stop mentally, to question what we have in front of us, to ask ourselves the origin of things, how they arose, because they continue to be, what materials are used, how can you do to get closer to that so close.

One is his own architect, we model our life, according to how we relate to our environment, but also with other living beings, nothing exceeds us, the world is great but not so much, everything is a cycle, we come and go, focusing is essential and understanding what makes us good, bad … loving ourselves a little even if it costs, is also part of appreciating.

I hope that as humanity we change a little, we are more people, we distribute more love and less hate, we appreciate life.

The power of analyzing stories

I know, I know… This isn’t any app or anything you can use to help your writing, but this is the first step to find your writing style. Without one, you’d always feel like your work is missing something.

This is more like a writing tip, but I consider this to be more important than all the apps you could ever find because they won’t be writing your story: you will.

Don‚Äôt just listen to what other storytellers say you should do, figure out what you like about the stories you enjoy, and learn to replicate that.

Want to learn about pacing? Examine stories you think flow fantastically.

Want to learn about making a flattering description? Study your favorite author‚Äôs descriptions.

Want to learn about characterization? Critique your favorite characters.

Want to learn about foreshadowing? Explore how it‚Äôs done in stories where the plot twist blew your mind.

Storytellers giving advice to other storytellers is fantastic and useful, but you will never know something as thoroughly as you know the things you figure out for yourself.

And by analyzing the stories you love instead of listening blindly to some advice, you‚Äôll never be swayed by the bias of other writers and you‚Äôll never take in advice that‚Äôs suited for a story you wouldn‚Äôt enjoy writing.

Milanote

This allows you to make and personalize mood boards, to-do lists, write posts, add links to resources, add Spotify playlist, create boards within boards, add photos, and documents; sends you reminders when a certain task has to be done… and much more.

Besides writing I use it to organize my commissions, school, and finances. The Milanote itself even has plenty of templates for almost everything, and I store almost anything in it these days. My family trees, interactive maps, notes, random ideas, character profiles.

It‚Äôs super easy to use and perfect if you are looking for something to make your story bible in. The app is available for both iOS and Android and any computer. You can also invite another person in and share your documents and your notes.

The only catch probably is that Milanote only allows you to add a maximum of 200 objects for free. Maybe those are enough for you?

Manuskript

This one right here I adore. And it’s FREE. Organized in importance, general info, characters, part of the plot/subplot, then resolution steps. Also, there‚Äôs a button on the right side so if you want to have the book summary on the screen you can.

Characters sorted into main/secondary/minor for your convenience. You can choose colors for them, which will be useful later… if it is for you.

You may have some world-building to do? Good cause there‚Äôs a part for this too.

Libre Office

A great alternative to Microsoft Office. Also, along with word processing software, it includes spreadsheets and PowerPoint capabilities. One of its best features is its compatibility with Microsoft Word formats.

Calibre

I can tell I can’t live without this software anymore. Also FREE. Honestly, it’s so easy to use and I have published my three books successfully thanks to this.

As an indie writer, I have to design my covers, my back covers, and everything in between, but once you get used to it you master it and it gives such a wide knowledge of designs, creative editing, etc.

Lifehack Tips

If you use some software you like but can’t afford or don’t want to pay for the full program, there are lots of YouTubers who upload the full version and they work perfectly.
