Books I Want To Read- Part 2

Admin
24 Haziran 2020
0 Comments

Books I Want To Read- Part 2 Books I Want To Read- Part 2

Welcome back everybody! Today’s article is a continuation of another article I wrote back in April. Everyone seemed to like it and I’ve been looking at tons more interesting books so here’s the sequel!

The Glass Castle by Jeannette Walls

This was one of the books on the summer reading list two years ago but I didn’t read it. I’ve heard it’s an amazing memoir and I love personal real life stories so I want to check it out.

The Little Prince by Antoine de Saint-Exupery

This is one of the most famous books of all time- selling almost 150 million copies since its publication- and it about a little prince who explores the world. It sounds cute and is a short read.

A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas

Ah yes. This book. I’ve been warned about it, I’ve been recommended it, I’ve even read parts of it (and cringed). I want to understand what all the hype (and hate) is about.

Agnes Gray by Anne Bronte

I’ve read “Jane Eyre” and “Wuthuring Heights” by the other Bronte sisters, Charlotte and Emily. The lesser known “Agnes Gray” by Anne is another book on my list and uses one of my favorite storylines- the governess plot.

Crazy Rich Asians by Kevin Kwan

Another story I think we’ve all heard of, made famous by the trilogy and by the movie. I want to see if this book is as awesome as everyone says and it’s pretty high on my list of contemporary novels.

Me Before You by Jojo Mores

I watched part of the movie (with two actors I love, Emilia Clarke and Sam Claflin) and although the story is pretty criticized and I already know what happens, I would like to read the book.

To All The Boys I’ve Loved Before by Jenny Han

This is a very popular teen read that I’m kind of nervous to check out from the library or buy because my family will for sure judge me (and the bookstore clerk and the librarian). But I actually enjoyed the clips I watched from the movie and I like the plot idea, so why not read it?

Everything, Everything by Nicola Yoon

This book is also pretty famous among teens. It became a movie I think a year or two ago. It’s about this girl that has an illness and so she can’t leave her home but then she falls in love. Sounds kinda cliche but I heard it’s pretty good.

The Sun Is Also A Star by Nicola Yoon

Same author as “Everything, Everything”, Yoon writes about deportation and romance in this story. I heard the movie wasn’t all that great but the book seems to be everywhere and it’s probably a pretty light read.

The Hate U Give by Angle Thomas

Now this book, this is a book that I’ve heard is absolutely stunning, along with the film. Especially with regard to the racism and police brutality in the USA (where I live) at the moment, I think it’s a book I should read pretty soon, and I recommend it to you as well, even though I haven’t read it yet.

A Little Princes by Frances Hodgson Burnett

I read the adapted version of this sad but sweet story as a child so I know the plot but I kinda miss it- I loved the story when I was young and read it over and over again. The full version will be a nice walk down memory lane.

And Then There Were None by Agatha Christie

I read “Murder on the Orient Express” in 2018, I think, and I watched the movie, and I loved it. I want to read her other most famous novel and get some more mystery novels in this year.

Catch-22 by Joseph Heller

This book is pretty famous in the US, and is considered one of the greatest books ever. It’s set during WWII and I do enjoy historical fiction and satires so I hope it’s as good as everyone says.

The Grapes of Wrath by John Steinbeck

To be honest, I hated “East of Eden” by Steinbeck, except for the last 50 pages or so, and I really hope that this book is better. It’s about the Great Depression and farmers in Oklahoma so hopefully it’ll be more to my liking.

The Giver by Lois Lowry

Last but not least, “The Giver”! I remember this being on the reading list when I was kid but I never read it. It’s apparently a pretty light and easy read but a great book and I’ve read some passages already.

Hope everyone enjoyed that article! Do you have any book recommendations? Want personal recommendations? Have you read any of these books? Let me know on my Instagram, @thefantastically. Stay safe!
