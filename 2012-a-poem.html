2012 / a POEM 2012 / a POEM

I remember the day the World ended. We were standing in the school yard on our first break, our 6th grader minds were spinning. We were all both scared and exited, although we knew nothing were to happen. We were jumping around hugging eachother, as if we really expected this World to end. But who could have known that every moment following that morning, we would wish that it had.

That morning, 21 of December 2012, was the beginning of the end. We may not have realized it at the time, but the mayans were right all along. The ground did not crack from under us, yet we walked carefully through life after that moment, knowing that any misstep could lead to our demise.

Now almost 8 years later we are scattered across the country, some in rehab, some in college and some at a 9 to 5 that barely pays the bills. I realize that we have nothing in common anymore. I do not know you more than you know me. But I do know that on that morning, we were the happiest we have ever been. I know that at 15, just 2 years later, half of us had wanted to kill ourselves at some point. I know that when you were thrown out of your parents house at 17 you wished it all would have ended in 2012. I know that when your brother overdozed, you wished of happiness. And I know that we have been stuck in houses with abusive parents for so long that the only way out seems to be death. I know that in these past 8 years we have been abused, assaulted, raped. We have overdozed, been to rehab, tried to kill ourselves and so much more. We have failed school, skipped classes, cut our skin, starved ourselves. We have been told to be scared of the World we live in and honestly, we have wanted to die on more days than we have wanted to live. So I know that on some nights we still look up at the same stars wishing for the same things. You, laying on the pavement on the other side of town. Me, sitting alone in my car as the sun is setting. We may not have seen each other in years, but I know you sometimes look back at that morning in 6th grade, when our biggest concern was the end of the World. Oh what a delight that was.
